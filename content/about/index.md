# ELEKIN: Intelligent Engineering and Bioengineering for the Society of the Future  

**Area:** Engineering and Architecture (excluding Computer Science)  
**ERC panel code:** PE8_4 Computational Engineering  

---

##  Vision  
ELEKIN is an interdisciplinary research group driving the development of **intelligent, bioinspired engineering for the society of the future**.  
Our work addresses major social and technological challenges defined in **RIS3 Euskadi 2030** and **Horizon Europe**, with a strong commitment to **gender equality, sustainability, and responsible innovation**.  

---

##  Research Lines  
The groups activities are structured into six interconnected and complementary research lines:  

1. **Social and industrial applications (RIS3):** Smart industry, collaborative production, emissions control, and digital health.  
2. **Bio- and neuro-inspired computation:** Computational neuroscience, cell programming, high-performance and quantum computing.  
3. **Data Science in multiscale cyber-physical systems:** Macro-nano data, Big Data, deep learning, and intelligent biometric security.  
4. **Personalized Intelligent Systems (IS) and Multiscale Modeling (MM):** Bioinspired models, cloud computing, cobots, and creative STEM.  
5. **Smart health and production:** Personalized management of mental and physical health, and intelligent systems for an active, diverse, and aging population.  
6. **Sustainable development, equality, and innovation management:** Convergence of natural and artificial intelligence, sustainability, and equitable development.  

---

##  Themes and Objectives  
ELEKIN aims to **advance applied research and technology transfer** in three strategic areas:  
- **Smart Industry:** Bioindustry, collaborative production, multiscale emission control.  
- **Smart Health:** Cognitive well-being, personalized healthcare, computational neuroscience.  
- **Sustainable Development and Energy:** Biodiversity monitoring, smart energy consumption and self-production, and climate change mitigation.  

These areas are approached from a **holistic and interdisciplinary perspective**, integrating engineering, neuroscience, biology, and social sciences.  

---

##  Collaborations and Transfer  
ELEKIN collaborates with leading institutions such as **Cambridge Neuroscience**, **Tecnalia**, **CEIT**, **UC**, and **NTT Data**, developing real-world use cases with tangible social and economic impact.  
Its expertise is also transferred to the **educational and training domains**, through masters and doctoral programs, and partnerships with industry and social stakeholders.
